ViralCut

Automated viral video editing pipeline with beat-sync, captions, face tracking, effects, and Final Cut Pro export

Python 3.11+ MoviePy 2.x YOLO librosa faster-whisper FFmpeg PySceneDetect SAM2 OpenTimelineIO Pydantic

The Challenge

Editing viral short-form video is tedious, repetitive, and formulaic.

Viral content on TikTok, Reels, and Shorts follows predictable patterns: beat-synced cuts, zooms on faces, word-by-word captions, sound effects on transitions, silence removal, and speed ramps. Manually applying these edits in Final Cut Pro or Premiere takes hours per video, yet the rules are mechanical enough to automate.

A typical viral editing workflow involves: detecting scene boundaries, analyzing audio for beats and BPM, transcribing speech for captions, tracking faces for auto-zoom, choosing transitions and SFX, rendering the final video, and optionally exporting an FCPXML for further tweaking in Final Cut Pro. Each step requires a different tool or library. ViralCut unifies them into a single CLI pipeline.

Why It Matters

  • One-command editing: drop a raw video and an optional music track, get a fully edited MP4 and FCPXML in one pass. viralcut process input.mp4 --music track.mp3 --style viral-shorts
  • Beat-synced cuts: librosa extracts BPM and beat positions, then the decision engine places cuts on strong beats for rhythmic editing.
  • AI-powered analysis: YOLO detects faces for auto-zoom, faster-whisper transcribes speech for captions, PySceneDetect finds shot boundaries.
  • Style presets: YAML files define transition types, caption styles, SFX mappings, and cut rules. Swap a preset to change the entire edit aesthetic.
  • Dual export: renders a finished MP4 and exports FCPXML for Final Cut Pro, so editors can fine-tune the automated output.

Key Insights

Immutable Data Pipeline

Every stage produces a new Pydantic model, never mutating the previous one. Analysis produces AnalysisResult, decision produces EditDecisionList, and each can be serialized to JSON for caching or inspection. This makes every pipeline stage independently testable and debuggable.

Beat-Sync Algorithm

Cuts are placed on beat positions where beat.strength > threshold (default 0.5). Non-cut transitions (flash, whip, zoom) are randomly assigned with configurable probability (default 30%). This creates the rhythmic "on the beat" editing style that defines viral short-form content.

Graceful Optional Dependencies

Heavy ML dependencies are optional: faster-whisper (speech), ultralytics (faces), SAM2 + torch (tracking), rembg (background removal). Each analysis stage catches ImportError and logs a yellow "skipped" message. The pipeline degrades gracefully rather than failing.

Apple Silicon Aware

SAM2 runs on CPU only (MPS is broken for this model). YOLO uses MPS acceleration when available. The pipeline auto-detects the platform and routes each model to the optimal compute backend.

Dual Output Strategy

Rendering produces both a finished MP4 (for immediate publishing) and an FCPXML (for Final Cut Pro import). The FCPXML preserves all edit decisions as non-destructive metadata, so editors can tweak timing, swap transitions, or adjust captions without re-running the pipeline.
