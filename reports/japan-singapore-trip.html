Japan x Singapore

20-day trip planner: ETHTokyo conference, Japanese Alps road trip, Osaka food tour, and Singapore exploration with interactive Leaflet map, budget tracker, and packing checklist

HTML CSS Vanilla JS Leaflet.js Inter Font Unsplash Vercel

The Challenge

A 20-day trip spanning two countries, four phases, and mixing a blockchain conference with tourism requires a single source of truth.

The trip combines ETHTokyo (conference + hackathon), a rental car road trip through the Japanese Alps, an Osaka food and culture segment, and a Singapore finale. Each phase has different logistics, budgets, and packing needs. Spreadsheets fragment the planning. This site unifies everything into one shareable, interactive companion.

This multi-file vanilla web app (HTML + CSS + JS) presents the full 20-day journey on an interactive Leaflet map with a horizontal sticky timeline bar. Clicking a day pill updates the map view and shows the detail panel with activities, photos, and logistics. Separate sections cover budget breakdown by phase, weather expectations, and a persistent packing checklist with localStorage. A Singapore history modal adds cultural context. Two alternative road trip routes (A and B) are available with different itineraries loaded from separate data files.

Why It Matters

  • 4-phase color-coded journey: Tokyo (#5B6ABF), Road Trip (#C4813D), Osaka (#D4644E), Singapore (#3A8F7B). Each phase has its own accent color reflected in the timeline pills, map markers, and detail panel.
  • Dual route options: Route A and Route B for the road trip phase, loaded from separate JS data files. Different stops, different experiences, same start/end points.
  • Countdown timer: live countdown to departure date (Sept 22, 2026) displayed in the hero section.
  • Persistent packing checklist: checkboxes saved to localStorage. Pack your bags over multiple sessions without losing progress.
  • Budget breakdown: per-phase cost estimates with totals, displayed in a clean card layout.
  • Singapore history modal: timeline from 1299 (Singapura legend) through colonial era, Japanese occupation, independence, Lee Kuan Yew era, to modern city-state.

Key Insights

Phase-Based Color System

Each of the 4 trip phases has a distinct color that permeates the entire UI. Tokyo days appear in indigo (#5B6ABF), road trip days in warm amber (#C4813D), Osaka in terracotta (#D4644E), and Singapore in teal (#3A8F7B). The timeline pills, map markers, detail panel accents, and budget cards all respect phase colors. This makes it instantly clear which phase you are viewing.

Conference + Tourism Hybrid

This is not a pure tourism site. The Tokyo phase mixes ETHTokyo conference days (keynotes, workshops, networking) with hackathon days (build sprints, demos) and exploration days (temples, vintage districts). The data model supports this mix naturally, treating conference sessions and tourist activities as equal first-class entries in the day's activity list.

Dual Route Options

The road trip phase offers two alternative routes loaded from separate JS data files (route-b-data.js and route-tokyo-takayama.js). Route A goes through the Japanese Alps via Shirakawa-go and Matsumoto. Route B takes an alternative path via Takayama. Users can switch between routes to compare itineraries before committing.

Light Warm Aesthetic

Unlike the dark-themed Georgia and Iceland trip sites, Japan x Singapore uses a light warm palette: off-white (#FAFAF8) background, subtle borders (#E5E3DE), clean shadows. The design references Japanese minimalism. No hero overlay darkness, no dramatic gradients. Just clean Inter typography on warm paper tones.

Persistent Packing Checklist

The packing checklist uses localStorage to remember checked items across browser sessions. Categories (clothing, electronics, documents, toiletries) each have their own section. This practical feature turns the site from a planning tool into an active trip companion you use while actually packing your bags.
