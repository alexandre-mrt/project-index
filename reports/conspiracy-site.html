SlopEncyclopedia

Conspiracy theory encyclopedia with terminal/classified-document aesthetic. Built as a night-shift project with 5/5 functional verification.

Next.js 14 TypeScript strict Tailwind v4 D3.js Biome Bun Vercel

The Challenge

Conspiracy theories are scattered, sensationalized, and rarely cross-linked.

Wikipedia sections are locked down, YouTube rabbit-holes autoplay ads, and no canonical browser exists for exploring how theories connect — who claims what, which actors recur, which events get reused across narratives.

SlopEncyclopedia presents a curated dataset as a browsable archive with a distinctive classified/terminal aesthetic. Records are linkable, a D3 force-directed graph surfaces cross-theory connections, and full-archive search makes exploration fast.

Why It Matters

  • Structured over prose: every theory is a record with typed fields, not a blog post.
  • Force graph reveals connections: rectangle labeled nodes with interactive popups expose recurring actors, events, and themes.
  • Classified aesthetic: terminal-style UI mirrors the content and helps readers treat entries as artefacts rather than statements of fact.
  • Mobile-first: responsive popups, collapsible graph controls, depth meter — works on a phone during the commute.
