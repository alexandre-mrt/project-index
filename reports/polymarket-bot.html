Polymarket Bot

Autonomous multi-strategy trading bot for prediction markets

TypeScript Bun Polymarket CLOB Claude API Ethers.js SQLite Zod WebSocket Ink TUI

The Challenge

Prediction markets offer real alpha, but manual trading cannot scale.

Arbitrage windows last 2.7 seconds on average. News moves prices before a human can react. Emotional bias leads to overexposure. Tracking whale wallets, scanning hundreds of markets, and sizing positions with Kelly criterion all demand automation.

Polymarket hosts hundreds of active binary markets on politics, crypto, sports, and world events. Each market has a CLOB order book with real-time pricing. Profitable edges exist in mispriced outcomes, near-certain "bond" positions, news catalysts, and whale consensus, but exploiting them requires continuous scanning, sub-second execution, and disciplined risk management that no human can sustain 24/7.

Why It Matters

  • 9 concurrent strategies: arbitrage, bonds, copy-trading, news-driven, Claude edge, longshot attention, time decay, fade, and rewards, all running in parallel every 30 seconds.
  • AI probability assessment: Claude API reads full market descriptions and news signals to estimate true probabilities, catching edges that pure price analysis misses.
  • Kelly-optimal sizing: quarter-Kelly criterion with fee-adjusted effective prices ensures mathematically sound position sizing.
  • Kill switch protection: automatic circuit breaker on 20% drawdown, manual SIGINT trigger, and emergency order cancellation.
  • Paper + live modes: full simulation with realistic fee modeling before committing real capital.

Strategy pattern: all strategies implement BaseStrategy with evaluate(context) returning TradeSignal[]. The StrategyRunner executes all enabled strategies in parallel via Promise.all and deduplicates signals by tokenId + side, keeping the highest confidence.

Risk Management

Quarter-Kelly Sizing f = 0.25 * (p - effectivePrice) / (1 - effectivePrice) where: p = estimated true probability effectivePrice = market price + taker fee taker fee = feeRate * sqrt(price * (1 - price)) Position Limits Max per market: 10% of bankroll Max total exposure: 50% of bankroll Max drawdown: 20% (triggers kill switch) Kill Switch Drawdown >= 20% --> cancel all orders, halt loop SIGINT received --> emergency stop Manual activate --> immediate halt + callback chain

Key Insights

Multi-Strategy Parallelism

All 9 strategies evaluate in parallel every 30 seconds. The StrategyRunner wraps each in a try/catch so one failure never blocks the others. Signals are deduplicated by tokenId + side before reaching the executor.

Resolution Condition Safety

Never trade on the market title alone. The bot reads full description fields from the Gamma API to check exact resolution criteria, deadlines, 50-50 fallback clauses, and conditional market traps ("X before Y" resolves 50-50 if neither happens). A 95c outcome is only a "bond" if resolution conditions are unambiguous.

Fee-Aware Kelly Sizing

The Kelly criterion incorporates Polymarket's dynamic taker fee curve: fee = feeRate * sqrt(price * (1 - price)), peaking at ~1.80% for 50c outcomes. The bot never overestimates its edge on mid-priced markets where fees eat the most.

Graceful Shutdown

On SIGTERM/SIGINT, the bot follows a strict teardown sequence: stop scan loop, cancel all open orders via CLOB API, stop data feeds and whale tracker, dispose kill switch listeners, close SQLite database. A double-SIGINT forces immediate exit.

Paper Trading Fidelity

Paper mode simulates realistic taker fees at 150 bps, writes trades to the same SQLite schema as live mode, and runs the full portfolio/risk pipeline. Strategy validation happens without capital at risk while maintaining identical code paths.
