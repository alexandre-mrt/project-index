The Challenge

Major life decisions — changing jobs, managing conflict, handling debt, processing grief — are among the most consequential choices a person makes. Yet there is no tool that maps decision trees with structured, evidence-based guidance. People rely on gut feeling, scattered blog posts, or expensive professionals for situations that follow well-documented patterns.

Core problem: Life decisions are complex, interconnected, and high-stakes. Without a structured framework, people repeat the same mistakes, miss consequences they could have anticipated, and fail to see how one decision cascades into others.

Psychology research, behavioral economics, and conflict resolution literature contain evidence-based guidance for most common life situations. But this knowledge is locked in academic papers and 400-page books — not in a format anyone can use when they actually need it.

Why It Matters

  • Visual decision mapping — interactive graph shows how situations connect across domains, revealing cascading consequences before they happen
  • Evidence-based options — each decision node includes multiple reaction options with short-term and long-term consequences, backed by cited academic sources
  • Severity and importance scoring — nodes are rated by severity (low to critical) and importance (1-10), helping users prioritize what matters most
  • Bilingual support (EN/FR) — every situation, context, and option is available in both English and French, with locale switching built into the UI
  • Life ELO system — a gamified scoring model (baseline 1200) that computes penalties from active situations and recoveries from applying recommended moves, with tiers from "Thriving" (S+) down to "Cooked" (F)
  • Recommended moves — each node highlights the evidence-backed best option, making expert guidance instantly accessible

Key Insights

Decision graphs over decision lists. Life decisions do not exist in isolation. A career change affects finances, which affects relationship stress, which affects emotional health. The graph visualization makes these cascading connections visible and navigable, turning abstract complexity into something you can actually explore.

Evidence-based, not opinion-based. Every node cites academic sources — papers, books, articles with DOIs and ISBNs. Each option includes projected short-term and long-term consequences. The recommended option is not a guess; it is the approach most supported by research.

Bilingual from the data model up. Instead of bolting on i18n as an afterthought, every string in the data layer is a BilingualText { en, fr }. The locale provider switches the entire app — situations, options, consequences, subcategory labels — in one click.

Gamification through Life ELO. The ELO scoring system (baseline 1200) turns abstract life management into a measurable score. Active situations subtract points based on severity and importance. Applying recommended moves recovers 60% of the penalty. Tiers from "Thriving" (S+) to "Cooked" (F) make progress tangible and motivating.

Subcategory grouping with dagre auto-layout. The graph engine groups 72 nodes into collapsible subcategory clusters, auto-positioned by dagre. Filter by domain, subcategory, or minimum importance. Desktop gets full ReactFlow with minimap; mobile gets a responsive card list. No manual positioning needed.
