Iceland Trip

Epic 18-day Iceland ring road trip companion: Leaflet map with animated route, day-by-day itinerary with activity images, Icelandic history section, and packing guide

HTML CSS Vanilla JS Leaflet.js Playfair Display Inter Font Wikimedia

The Challenge

An Iceland ring road trip covers 3,000 km through some of the most extreme landscapes on Earth. Standard travel tools do not capture the scale or the detail.

You need to know which F-roads require a 4x4, where to find hot springs after a glacier hike, how to plan around unpredictable weather, and where to camp in a van. Google Maps shows roads but not context. Travel blogs bury practical info under paragraphs of prose. This site puts everything in one interactive, visually stunning package.

This single-page application covers an 18-day van road trip around Iceland. It features a Playfair Display serif-driven editorial design with glacier-blue accents on icy backgrounds. The Leaflet map shows the full ring road route with animated playback. Each of the 18 days gets an expanded card with activity rows, hover-to-preview side images, and tagged categories. A collapsible history section spans 1,100 years of Icelandic history from Viking colonization to modern volcanic eruptions.

Why It Matters

  • 18-day ring road itinerary: approximately 3,000 km in a 4x4 van, covering the Golden Circle, south coast, Eastfjords, Myvatn, Westfjords, and Snaefellsnes peninsula.
  • Rich visual identity: Playfair Display serif for headings, Inter for body text. Volcanic dark (#1A1A2E), glacier blue (#4CC9F0), aurora green (#06D6A0), lava red (#E84545). The design evokes fire and ice.
  • Activity-level granularity: each day card lists individual stops with icons (adventure, culture, food, drive, worship), and hovering shows a side image with a description overlay.
  • Icelandic history timeline: 6 eras from Viking settlement (874) through the Althing (930), foreign domination, the catastrophic Laki eruption (1783), independence (1944), to modern volcanic activity. Illustrated with Wikimedia Commons photos.
  • Highlights gallery: curated photo cards of the most dramatic stops with hover-zoom effects.

Key Insights

Fire and Ice Design Language

The color palette mirrors Iceland's geological duality. Volcanic dark (#1A1A2E) for backgrounds, glacier blue (#4CC9F0) for accents and CTAs, aurora green (#06D6A0) for culture markers, lava red (#E84545) for adventure markers. The Playfair Display serif typeface adds an editorial gravitas that matches the epic scale of the landscape.

Day Cards with Side Image Preview

Each of the 18 day cards shows a full-width hero photo at the top, then splits into two columns: activity rows on the left and a side image panel on the right. Hovering over an activity row swaps the side image and shows a description overlay. This pattern keeps cards compact (no vertical scroll needed per day) while providing visual context for every stop.

1,100 Years of History

The collapsible history section covers 6 eras: Viking colonization (874), the Althing parliament (930), Norwegian/Danish domination (1262-1550), the catastrophic Laki eruption (1783), independence (1944), and modern Iceland (Cod Wars, 2008 crash, Eyjafjallajokull, Fagradalsfjall). Each era has a Wikimedia photo and an alternating left-right layout for visual rhythm.

Responsive Without a Framework

A single @media (max-width: 900px) breakpoint restructures the map panel from horizontal to vertical, hides the nav links for mobile, and adjusts the timeline and card layouts. No Tailwind, no Bootstrap, no media query library. Just 20 lines of CSS overrides that handle the mobile experience cleanly.
