Portfolio Construction

Advanced portfolio optimization comparing risk-based strategies on crypto and traditional asset portfolios

Python NumPy Pandas SciPy Matplotlib riskfolio-lib

The Challenge

How do you build a diversified portfolio when half the assets are cryptocurrencies with extreme volatility and non-normal return distributions?

Traditional mean-variance optimization (Markowitz) assumes elliptical distributions and stable correlations. Crypto assets violate both assumptions. The SBF crash of November 2022 demonstrated how correlation structures shift violently during crises, making naive optimization dangerous.

This project implements and compares 5 portfolio construction strategies across 14 assets (10 crypto-USD pairs + 4 traditional indices) over the period October 2017 to March 2025. It uses cleaned covariance matrices (both sample and EWMA), performs out-of-sample backtesting with periodic rebalancing, and evaluates each strategy by Euler risk contributions and diversification metrics.

Why It Matters

  • 5 competing strategies: 1/N (equal weight), Markowitz (minimum variance), Roncalli ERC (equal risk contribution), Meucci (minimum effective number of bets), and DePrado HRP (hierarchical risk parity).
  • Covariance cleaning: both raw sample and EWMA-smoothed covariance matrices are pre-computed and cleaned, then loaded for each optimization window.
  • Out-of-sample backtesting: weights are computed on historical windows (PP_252, TR_252, etc.) and applied forward to unseen returns, preventing look-ahead bias.
  • Crash resilience analysis: the dataset spans the SBF/FTX collapse, enabling comparison of how each strategy handles regime changes.

Key Insights

ERC Sanity Checking

The Roncalli ERC optimizer includes a post-optimization sanity check. After SLSQP converges, it computes the spread between maximum and minimum absolute risk contributions. If the spread exceeds the tolerance (default 1e-6), it raises an assertion error. This catches silent optimization failures where the solver reports success but the solution is not truly equal-risk.

EWMA vs Sample Covariance

Both raw sample and exponentially-weighted moving average covariance matrices are pre-computed for each period. The ERC and Markowitz optimizers preferentially load EWMA matrices (which give more weight to recent observations), falling back to sample covariance if the EWMA file is not found. HRP uses the standard cleaned covariance for its correlation-based clustering.

Out-of-Sample Framework

The build_performance_slices() function in the orchestrator carefully handles period boundaries. Weights computed at date key PP_252 are applied to returns from that date until the next rebalancing point. Boundary dates are deduplicated, and the full performance series is reindexed to the total returns timeline with zero fill for gaps.

Modular Strategy Design

Each optimization strategy is a self-contained class (Roncalli_pf, Markowitz_pf, DePrado_pf, Meucci_pf) that loads its own data, runs optimization, and exposes a .weights attribute. The orchestrator does not need to know how each optimizer works internally; it only calls the constructor and reads the result. This makes it trivial to add new strategies.
