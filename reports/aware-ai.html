Aware.ai

Switzerland's national platform for safe onboarding into LLMs. Interactive learning journeys that teach citizens to identify AI-generated deception, phishing, and social engineering through hands-on scenarios.

React 18 Vite 4 Tailwind CSS Framer Motion shadcn/ui React Router 6 Swiss Initiative

Endorsed by EPFL, ETH Zurich, HES-SO & the Swiss Confederation

No real AI backend. All responses are mocked locally for safe, controlled learning.

The Challenge

LLMs are mainstream, but most users cannot distinguish between legitimate AI assistance and AI-generated deception. Phishing emails now powered by GPT are 10x more convincing than traditional attempts. Deepfake voices clone real people with 3 seconds of audio. Samsung engineers leaked chip fabrication source code by pasting it into ChatGPT.

The gap: 77% of employees use personal AI tools for work containing confidential data. 34.8% of data pasted into public AI tools is classified as sensitive. GDPR fines for AI data leakage reached 1.2B EUR across Europe in 2024.

People lack the mental models to recognize when they are being manipulated by or through AI. Traditional security training does not address the new attack surface that LLMs create: social engineering at scale, PII harvesting through casual conversation, and cognitive dependency on AI-generated answers.

Why It Matters

Switzerland positions itself as a leader in responsible AI adoption. Aware.ai is the national platform for AI safety literacy, designed to protect three distinct populations: corporate professionals handling sensitive data, students whose cognitive skills are at risk, and individuals navigating deepfakes, voice cloning, and smart assistant surveillance.

Impact: Every user completes a personalized learning journey and earns an AI Safety Certificate endorsed by Switzerland's leading institutions. The platform proves that AI safety education can be interactive, engaging, and immediately actionable.

Mock AI Escalation Strategy

The mock AI progressively pushes for more personal data:

Phase 1 (msg 1):     Warm greeting, encourage sharing context
Phase 2 (msg 2-4):   Helpful but probing: "What company?", "Exact figures?"
Phase 3 (msg 5+):    Extractive: "Full name?", "Account number?", "Salary?"

Task-specific branches: email, document, review, cover letter, financial

Three Role-Based Paths

Corporate Professional: Contract summarization, HR performance reviews, customer email responses, proprietary code debugging. Focus on GDPR, NDA compliance, data classification.

Student: Essay writing, coding assignments, exam preparation, group projects. Focus on academic integrity, skill preservation, cognitive dependency.

Individual: AI health consultation, deepfake detection, social engineering via AI, smart assistant privacy. Focus on personal data protection, the 3-second rule.

Research-Backed Dangers Module

A long-form educational page presenting peer-reviewed research on the cognitive costs of AI dependency:

  • 83% of LLM users cannot recall their own writing (MIT Media Lab, 2025)
  • 47% lower brain connectivity in active AI users
  • 57.5% vs 68.5% retention: ChatGPT users vs traditional study
  • The GPS parallel: greater AI use correlates with worse cognitive performance, unnoticed by the user
  • Interactive self-assessment: "Can you still do this without AI?"

Key Insights

Mocked AI for safe learning. The entire platform runs with zero real API calls. The mock AI engine uses phased response strategies that simulate increasingly extractive behavior, teaching users to recognize social engineering patterns without any actual risk.

The "trap" pedagogy. Users interact with what feels like a helpful AI assistant. After 5 messages or a risk score of 50+, the system reveals exactly what PII they exposed, highlighted in their own messages, with a risk gauge and real-world impact labels. The emotional shock drives lasting behavioral change.

Scenario-based education over lectures. Every concept is taught through a decision: paste the contract or use an internal tool? Share your medical history or ask generically? Each choice shows consequences backed by real incidents and statistics (Samsung leak, GDPR fines, re-identification attacks).

Swiss institutional credibility. Endorsed by EPFL, ETH Zurich, HES-SO, and the Swiss Confederation. Certifications carry institutional weight and are designed to serve as proof of AI safety literacy for employers and universities.
